## The Right Mindset

"I'd treat it like a junior operator, not some magic money printer" — this framing flips the whole conversation. Hermes isn't going to deposit money in your bank account while you sleep. But it will execute the tedious, repeatable parts of client acquisition.

> *"If I wanted Hermes to pay for itself, I'd treat it like a junior operator, not some magic money printer. Find 25 B2B companies that could benefit from a content system, capture name/site/why-they-need-it, draft three message variants for review."*
>
> — **Sharbel A.** on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2WZAcWtwoDI)

---

### The 5-Workflow System

| Workflow | What Hermes Does | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------------|------------|
| Prospect research | Find 25 B2B companies that need a content system | 5+ hrs |
| Lead capture | Extract name, site, why-they-need-it, contact info | 2 hrs |
| Message drafting | Write 3 variant outreach messages per prospect | 3 hrs |
| Follow-up scheduling | Auto-schedule follow-ups based on response | 1 hr |
| Pipeline tracking | Maintain a CRM sheet/DB of all prospects | 30 min |

### Why the "Junior Operator" Frame Works

A junior employee costs $3,000-5,000/month. Hermes costs a fraction of that. But the expectations should match: you don't hand a junior a vague goal and expect millions. You give them a structured process with review gates.

---

## Setup

1. **Define your target client** — Industry, size, pain point (e.g., "B2B SaaS companies that need content marketing")
2. **Build the research skill** — Web search for companies matching your criteria
3. **Create the capture skill** — Scrape site metadata, extract pain points
4. **Write the message skill** — Draft variants (cold email, LinkedIn DM, Twitter)
5. **Set up a CRM skill** — Supabase, Airtable, or Google Sheets to track pipeline
6. **Schedule daily prospecting** — Cron job to find 5 new prospects per day
7. **Review and approve** — Hermes drafts, you send
