## The Briefing That Gets Faster Every Week

Most tools degrade without maintenance. @aakashgupta discovered the opposite: his competitive briefing prompt stayed the same for 6 weeks, but the output quality improved and the time dropped from 20 minutes to 8 — because Hermes learned his preferences, remembered what mattered, and got faster at the task.

> *"My competitive briefing went from 20 min to 8 min over 6 weeks. Same prompt."*
>
> — **@aakashgupta** on [X/Twitter](https://x.com/aakashgupta/status/2050349093969694825)

### The Compounding Improvement Curve

| Week | Time | Quality |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | 20 min | Baseline |
| 2 | 17 min | Better |
| 4 | 12 min | Good |
| 6 | 8 min | Excellent |

### Why This Happens

Hermes's memory system means every week adds to what it knows about your preferences, competitors, and industry context. The same prompt produces better output because the underlying knowledge base is richer. This is the opposite of prompt decay — it's prompt appreciation.

---

## How You Can Do This Too

1. **Write one good briefing prompt**
2. **Run it weekly** — Same prompt, same format
3. **Let memory accumulate** — Don't clear or reset your agent
4. **Track the time** — Notice when it starts getting faster
5. **Review monthly** — Tweak the prompt only when needed
