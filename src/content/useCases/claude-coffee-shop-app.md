### What This Means

The zero-code narrative has floated around for years but proof has been thin. This is a documented transaction for a custom business application with a real client. The path from prompt to paid invoice is now short enough that a single person can close it in days, not months.

The amount matters too. $700 is not life-changing, but it is real revenue from real work. That shift unlocks a new category of micro-consulting where the barrier to entry is prompt skill, not programming skill.

### How to Replicate This

The pattern is repeatable this week:

1. **Pick a local business** with a clear operational need: inventory tracking, order management, booking system, menu management.
2. **Use Claude or another AI coding agent** to build a functional prototype. Describe what you want in plain language, iterate on what the AI produces.
3. **Charge a flat fee** that covers your iteration time and the business value delivered. Start with small businesses that would never hire a developer at market rates.
4. **Deliver and move on.** The agent does not replace the sales conversation or the requirement gathering. It replaces the months of implementation time.

The question isn't whether the code is perfect. It is whether the app works well enough for a single storefront to pay for it. In this case, the answer was yes.

### The Bigger Picture

This story signals something larger than one sale. It shows that AI coding agents have crossed a threshold: they can produce production-quality work for simple business applications, and non-technical people can use them to generate real income. The micro-consulting wave is just beginning.
